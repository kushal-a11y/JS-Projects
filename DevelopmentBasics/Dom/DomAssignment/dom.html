  • Shahada (Declaration of Faith): This pillar involves a declaration of belief in the oneness of God (Allah) and in Muhammad as His final prophet. It's a fundamental statement of faith that distinguishes a Muslim from other religions
  • Salah (Prayer): Muslims are required to pray five times a day, facing the direction of the Kaaba in Mecca. These prayers are a form of direct communication with God and a way to remember and honor Him throughout the day.
  • Zakat (Almsgiving): Zakat is a form of charity or almsgiving, where Muslims are expected to give a portion of their wealth to those in need. It's a way to purify one's wealth and to help those less fortunate.
  • Sawm (Fasting): During the month of Ramadan, Muslims abstain from food, drink, and other physical pleasures from dawn to sunset. Fasting is a time of spiritual reflection, discipline, and empathy for those who are struggling.
  • Hajj (Pilgrimage): The Hajj is a pilgrimage to Mecca that all able Muslims are expected to make at least once in their lifetime. It involves a series of rituals and ceremonies that symbolize unity, humility, and devotion to God.
